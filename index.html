A article written discussing whether a high school student should consider taking a job as an extracurricular based on their future aspirations and for college applications.

As a high school student, I often grappled with whether job experience holds merit. To shed light on this question, I will address the significance of job experience and how it aligns with individual career aspirations.

To start off, I will give an example of two students with different plans for the future. Student A, plans on majoring in Business and would like to open a Business one day. In this case job experience might be essential as it provides Student A with the necessary knowledge of how to run a Business which many jobs do provide. On the other hand, Student B is an avid Computer Science student who plans on working in software engineering. In this case, a internship may provide Student B with the experience and readiness needed when Student B enters job market.
